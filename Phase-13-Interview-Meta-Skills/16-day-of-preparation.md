# Day-of Preparation

## 0ï¸âƒ£ Prerequisites

Before diving into day-of preparation, you should understand:

- **All Previous Topics**: You've done the preparation work (Topics 1-15)
- **Mock Interview Experience**: You've practiced multiple times

Quick refresher: The day of the interview is not the time to learn new material. It's about optimizing your mental and physical state to perform at your best. Think of it like an athlete before a big game: the training is done, now it's about being ready to execute.

---

## 1ï¸âƒ£ Why Day-of Preparation Matters

Even well-prepared candidates can underperform on interview day due to:

- **Poor sleep**: Cognitive function drops significantly with sleep deprivation
- **Anxiety**: Nervousness can block recall and clear thinking
- **Technical issues**: Environment problems waste precious time
- **Low energy**: Blood sugar crashes affect performance
- **Rushing**: Last-minute scrambling creates stress

Proper day-of preparation ensures you can access the knowledge you've built.

```

   - Functional: What features?
   - Non-functional: Scale, latency, consistency
   - Out of scope

   - QPS = Daily actions / 100,000
   - Storage = Items Ã— Size Ã— Time
   - Peak = Average Ã— 3

   - Draw architecture
   - Explain components
   - Show data flow

   - Pick complex component
   - Algorithms, data structures
   - Failure handling

5. WRAP-UP (5 min)
   - Trade-offs
   - Monitoring
   - Future improvements
```

## 7ï¸âƒ£ During the Interview

### First 2 Minutes

```
THE CRITICAL FIRST 2 MINUTES:

1. GREET WARMLY
   - Smile (even on video)
   - "Hi [Name], nice to meet you"
   - Brief small talk if they initiate

2. LISTEN TO THE PROBLEM
   - Don't interrupt
   - Take notes
   - Note any specific requirements mentioned

3. TAKE A BREATH
   - Don't rush to answer
   - 5-10 seconds of thought is fine
   - "Great question, let me think about this"

4. START WITH STRUCTURE
   - "Before I dive in, I'd like to clarify requirements"
   - Shows you have a framework
   - Sets professional tone
```

## 8ï¸âƒ£ Between Interviews (Full Day)

If you have multiple interviews in one day:

```
BETWEEN INTERVIEW ROUTINE (10-15 min breaks):

1. PHYSICAL RESET
   - Use bathroom
   - Drink water
   - Light snack if hungry
   - Stretch / walk around

2. MENTAL RESET
   - Don't analyze previous interview
   - Clear your mind
   - Each interview is independent
   - Fresh start mentality

3. PREPARE FOR NEXT
   - Quick glance at framework
   - Deep breath
   - Positive self-talk
   - Ready for next interviewer

DON'T:
- Obsess over mistakes from previous interview
- Skip breaks to prepare more
- Consume heavy food
- Check phone/email (distracting)
```

Day-of preparation is about optimizing your state to perform at your best. The training is done; now it's about execution. Get good sleep, eat well, do a light warm-up, and manage anxiety with breathing and visualization.

Set up your environment perfectly: test everything, have backups ready, and eliminate distractions. Join early, greet warmly, and start with your framework.

Trust your preparation. You've done the work. Now show them what you know.
